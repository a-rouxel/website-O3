Terminology & Concepts

O3 Concepts

Key terminology and concepts used in orbital operations orchestration, from mission planning to execution monitoring.

Orbital Operations

Operations performed by spacecraft in orbit, including maneuvers, observations, communications, and maintenance activities. Each operation has temporal, resource, and constraint requirements that must be satisfied.

Orchestration

The coordination of multiple operations across time and resources to achieve mission objectives while respecting physical, temporal, and operational constraints. Orchestration involves planning, scheduling, and real-time execution management.

Constraint Satisfaction

Temporal Constraints

Time windows, deadlines, and sequencing requirements that operations must satisfy. Examples include launch windows, communication windows, and observation opportunities.

Resource Constraints

Limitations on power, fuel, data storage, and other consumables that affect which operations can be performed and when.

Physical Constraints

Orbital mechanics, pointing requirements, and hardware limitations that determine feasible operation sequences.

Operational Constraints

Mission rules, safety protocols, and operational procedures that must be followed during execution.

Mission Planning

The process of determining what operations need to be performed to achieve mission objectives. Planning considers long-term goals, available resources, and environmental factors to generate operation sequences.

Operation Scheduling

The assignment of specific times and resources to planned operations. Scheduling resolves conflicts, optimizes resource utilization, and ensures all constraints are satisfied while maximizing mission value.
